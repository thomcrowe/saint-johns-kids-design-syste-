Design System · Saint John's Episcopal · Tulsa

The children's ministry identity — bright, playful, and welcoming. For infants through 5th grade, and the families around them.

01 · The Idea

Joyful, generous, full of color

Kids is the warm heart of the family identity. The rounded lowercase saintjohn's wordmark with its overlapping translucent letters spells out exactly what the ministry feels like: many colors, gently layered, better together. The KIDS descriptor sits vertically in gray at the right. Where Youth grows up, Kids stays open-handed and bright — like the sower scattering seed everywhere.

Logo rule: the mark shown above is your actual logo art, embedded from St_Johns_Kids_Logo.png (master vector: St_Johns_Kids_Logo.ai). It is fixed art — never redraw, retype, or recolor the letters. Always place the real file; the translucent letter overlaps are the signature and can't be reconstructed.

Kids vs. Youth: same spectrum, opposite defaults. Kids uses pastels on white/cream; Youth uses deepened colors on ink. That single flip is what tells the two ministries apart at a glance while keeping them obviously family.

03 · Liturgical Calendar

One color per season

Children notice when the frontals and vestments change — the Children's Chapel lessons already lean on color and season. Wearing the season's color on Kids collateral quietly teaches the church year. Same mapping as Youth, pitched in the softer pastels.

05 · The Geometry

Confetti corners

Where Youth frames the page with a few big triangles, Kids scatters them — clustered pastel triangles in the corners, paired with small dot trios and thin diagonal hatching, exactly like the logo-sheet backgrounds. Playful abundance is the whole point.

Do

  • Cluster 3–5 triangles per corner, varied sizes
  • Sprinkle dot trios (• • •) and light hatching
  • Use the full spectrum freely and joyfully
  • Keep white/cream space in the center for legibility

    • Don't

  • Recolor or reletter the wordmark
  • Use the dark ink canvas — that's Youth's
  • Let confetti crowd the text unreadable
  • Go monochrome — Kids should always feel colorful

    07 · Voice

    Warm, wonder-filled, faithful

    Matching the Children's Chapel curriculum and Fr. Thom's family emails: gentle and inviting, full of wonder, and unafraid of real faith. It speaks to children directly and to parents warmly — sacramental language stays, just told simply, like a parable.

    Sounds right

  • "Let anyone with ears listen!"
  • "Come give them a real, hands-on week with Christ."
  • "Ask God to make your heart good soil."

    • Off-brand

  • Dumbed-down or preachy talking-down
  • Faith stripped out to feel "neutral"
  • Corporate program-speak with no wonder
